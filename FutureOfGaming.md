# The future of online gaming and NFTs

### my views as an **indie dev**.

Did you know that in order to publish your game on the steam platform, you must pay 100 dollars as an "admin fee" AND steam takes 30% of your sales along with exclusivity to steam.
This might sound horrible, but steam kind of invented the indie dev space with their steam platform. With the use of wishlists and your games store page,
indie devs now could market their games incredibly easy. Steam was released in \*1 **2003** (twenty years ago) But with time, the internet changed and the game development process changed with it. Now you have Unity and Unreal engine, and it has become exponentially easier to release and make money of your game as an indie dev.
During this time steam has had monopoly on this kind of service, and that is why it costs so much to publish on steam. They could simply just tell you to GTFO and you would not be
able to release your game.
**BUT** there is a new contender in town!! Unreal has announced and opened their beta for their own platform! And their prices are too good to be true. As a developer on Unreal you get to keep 88% of your games income.

> **Note:** This should not be concidered financial advice. Always do your own research.
